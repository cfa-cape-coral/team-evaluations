How This Evaluation Works

Your score comes from two things: how reliable you've been with following policies, and how engaged you've been with going above and beyond. We use something called "band-based scoring" instead of a strict formula, which basically means we group employees by their infraction patterns rather than treating every point the same way.

Step 1: Calculate Your PPM
PPM = Total Infraction Points ÷ Months Worked

PPM stands for Points Per Month. If you worked 6 months and got 60 infraction points total, your PPM is 10. This lets us compare people fairly even if they've been here different amounts of time.

Step 2: Find Your Base Score

Once we know your PPM, we use this table to find your base reliability score. Lower PPM = higher base score. This isn't a straight line—it's set up based on how the whole team actually performs.

What "Band-Based" Means

Most team members have very low PPM (0-25 range). Instead of spreading scores evenly across all possibilities, the bands are designed so that small differences in low PPM don't matter as much. The system focuses on separating people who are doing great from people who need serious improvement.

Example: If you have 5 PPM or 10 PPM, you're both in the same band (85 base). But if you jump from 100 PPM to 150 PPM, that's a bigger drop (78 to 75) because it shows a pattern getting worse.
